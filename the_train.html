Take a Trip Back in Time

Established in 1961 to preserve the island’s trains, the RPSI is a cross-border organisation with an office in Dublin and its headquarters in Whitehead , County Antrim. They have a total of 5 engines and 18 carriages. The Dublin Santa Special uses two of these engines, the No. 85 and the No. 4

No. 85 - Merlin

Looking a lot like Thomas the Tank Engine, the No. 85 was the original “Enterprise”. Introduced in 1941, and operated by the GNR (Great Northern Railway), it was a non-stop service between Dublin and Belfast.


Year:

1934

Built by:

Craven Brothers, Manchester and G.N.R. Dundalk

Weight:

48 Tonnes

No. 4 - The Beast

Built in Germany in 1926, the No. 4 is the pride of the RPSI. Rebuilt in the early 2010's in our Whitehead workshop, this mammoth of an engine can reach speeds of up to 100mph. Not bad for a near century old machine!


Year:

1926

Built by:

Siemens, Germany

Weight:

56 Tonnes

Craven Carriages

Most of our carriages originally came into service in the late 50's and early 60's. They were built by the famous Craven Brothers in Manchester, and ran up the mid-1990's before being retired by Irish Rail and NIR, and then taken over by the RPSI.


They’re quite different to their modern counterparts; the doorway between carriages needs a stooped head and good balance, there is a sliding window at each seat, and to open the doors you must first open the window, reach out, and then turn the handle from the outside.
